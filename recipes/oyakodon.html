Oyakodon (Chicken and Egg Rice Bowl)

Description

This is another go-to recipe of mine for busy weekdays involving quick preparation and simple ingredients. "Donburi," or rice bowl meals, were one of my first introductions to Japanese cooking, and I love their simplicity. Make sure you put on a pot of high-quality short-grain rice and when it's all ready, pour a little extra sauce onto your bowl. You won't regret it.

Recipe from Just One Cookbook

Ingredients

  • 1/2 onion, peeled and sliced lengthwise
  • 10 oz boneless skinless chicken thighs, trimmed and cut into strips
  • 1 tbsp sake
  • 3-4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup dashi*
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • Cooked rice
  • Green onions, finely chopped, for garnish

*Tip: Dashi is Japanese soup stock. You can buy it in powder or packet form at Asian grocery stores or online. Or you can, make your own.

Steps

  1. Combine dashi, soy sauce, mirin, and sugar in a bowl or liquid measuring cup and mix until the sugar is dissolved.
  2. On a tray, combine chicken and sake. Set aside for 5 minutes to marinate.
  3. Crack eggs into a bowl and whisk briefly and gently until yolks are just broken and whites are "cut," forming a marble pattern. Do not beat the eggs!
  4. With heat off, add onions and liquid mixture into a pan. Turn heat to medium low and simmer. Once simmering, add chicken on top of the onions. Cook uncovered for 5 minutes or until chicken is no longer pink and onions are tender, flipping the chicken halfway through.
  5. Turn heat to medium. Drizzle the eggs in a circular pattern over the chicken and onions, avoiding the edges of the pan (the eggs will overcook). Cook until the eggs are still runny but just set.
  6. Serve over rice topped with chopped green onions, if desired.
